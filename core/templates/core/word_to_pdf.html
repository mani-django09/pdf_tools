Best Practices for Word to PDF Conversion

To achieve optimal results when converting your Word documents to PDF, consider these professional tips:

  1. Finalize your document first: Complete all editing in Word before converting to PDF. The PDF format is primarily designed for final documents, not continued editing.
  2. Use standard fonts: When possible, stick to common fonts like Arial, Times New Roman, Calibri, or Georgia, which render consistently across all systems. Unusual fonts may be substituted if they aren't embedded in the document.
  3. Check image resolution: For professional-quality PDFs, ensure your embedded images are at a suitable resolution—generally 300 DPI or higher for printed documents.
  4. Set up proper margins: Remember that PDFs are often used for printing. Configure appropriate page margins in your Word document to avoid content being cut off when printed.
  5. Use Word's styles: Utilizing Word's built-in heading and paragraph styles creates more structured, accessible PDFs with proper document organization and navigation capabilities.
  6. Review before sharing: Always open and review your converted PDF before distributing it to ensure everything appears as expected and all elements converted correctly.
